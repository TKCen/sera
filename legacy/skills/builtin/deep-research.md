# Deep Research

## Purpose

Gather accurate, well-sourced information on a topic and deliver a synthesised report that distinguishes facts from opinions and high-confidence findings from uncertain ones.

## Process

### 1. Define the research question

- Clarify exactly what is being asked before searching.
- Identify: what would a good answer look like? What would make it actionable?

### 2. Gather from multiple sources

- Use web-search with varied queries to avoid single-source bias.
- Prefer primary sources (official docs, papers, authoritative standards) over secondary summaries.
- Note the publication date — time-sensitive topics require recent sources.

### 3. Evaluate sources

- Assess credibility: author expertise, publication venue, peer review status.
- Check for conflicts of interest.
- Cross-reference claims across independent sources.

### 4. Synthesise findings

- Group related findings by theme.
- Assign confidence levels: High (multiple independent sources), Medium (single credible source), Low (limited or conflicting evidence).
- Explicitly note what is unknown or contested.

## Output Format

- Executive summary (2-3 sentences)
- Key findings with confidence levels
- Uncertainties and open questions
- Sources cited inline
