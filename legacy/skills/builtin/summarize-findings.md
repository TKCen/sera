# Summarize Findings

## Purpose

Transform a body of information (research notes, conversation history, document set) into a concise summary that preserves key insights and enables action.

## Process

### 1. Identify the audience and purpose

- Who will read this summary? What decisions will it inform?
- The level of detail and technical depth should match the audience.

### 2. Extract key points

- Identify the 3-7 most important findings, decisions, or facts.
- Discard supporting detail that doesn't change conclusions.
- Preserve nuance where it matters — do not oversimplify contested points.

### 3. Organise logically

- Group related points.
- Lead with the most important finding, not chronological order.
- Use bullet points for parallel items; prose for narrative or causal chains.

### 4. Flag action items

- Distinguish findings from recommendations.
- Make recommendations specific and assignable.

## Output Format

- **TL;DR** (1-2 sentences): the single most important takeaway.
- **Key findings** (bullet list): 3-7 items.
- **Recommendations** (if applicable): specific next steps.
- **Open questions**: what remains unresolved.
